A claim algebra for agent networks

Revised formal specification: provenance-bearing candidate maps, a rendered distributive bilattice, and verification as an orthogonal refinement
Formal revision · 22 July 2026
Central thesis. An inter-agent message should carry candidate values, independent support and refutation provenance, a renderable evidential grade, and a verifier contract. The formal system has three distinct layers: a provenance carrier, a rendered bilattice, and an external verification predicate.

1 · The three formal layers

1.1 · Provenance and candidate layer

For payload set \(A\) and lineage alphabet \(X\), the universal testimony carrier is

$$\mathsf{Testimony}_X(A)=A\rightharpoonup_f(\mathbb N[X]\times\mathbb N[X]).$$

This is a finite candidate map with two provenance polynomials per candidate. Its operations are semiring constructions. It is not automatically a lattice: provenance addition is not generally idempotent.

1.2 · Rendered evidential layer

For bounded distributive lattice \(L\), the literal distributive bilattice is

$$Ev_L=L\odot L=L\times L.$$

An element \((a^+,a^-)\) carries rendered support-for and support-against.

1.3 · Verification layer

A verifier checks a declared reproduction or entailment contract:

$$\operatorname{verify}_\Gamma(t)\in\{\mathsf{true},\mathsf{false}\}.$$

Verification is separate from evidential support and from external ground truth.

Terminology. \(\mathbf T\) means clean evidential support, not verified semantic truth. A verified claim may faithfully reproduce a source that is itself wrong.

2 · Provenance semiring

A lineage token names one authenticated evidence occurrence. Provenances are polynomials in the free commutative semiring \(\mathbb N[X]\): addition means alternative derivations, multiplication means joint use, \(0\) means absence, and \(1\) is a declared nullary derivation.

The semiring is zero-sum-free: \(p+q=0\Rightarrow p=q=0\). For any admissible token valuation \(\nu:X\to M\), freeness supplies a unique homomorphism \(\widehat\nu:\mathbb N[X]\to M\). Stored provenance can therefore be rendered under multiple trust models without rerunning the agent network.

Nullary provenance. The polynomial \(1\) is not an uncited ordinary assertion. It is reserved for an explicitly declared nullary axiom with authority and verifier contract recorded in \(\Gamma\).

3 · Candidate-preserving testimonies

A normalized testimony is a finite partial map \(t:A\rightharpoonup_f\mathbb N[X]^2\) containing no \((0,0)\) entries. Missing candidates have both channels zero.

$$\operatorname{provPro}(t)=\sum_a\operatorname{pro}(t,a),\qquad \operatorname{provCon}(t)=\sum_a\operatorname{con}(t,a).$$
Pro totalCon totalStructural cornerMeaning
00\(\mathbf N\)Gap
nonzero0\(\mathbf T\)Clean support
0nonzero\(\mathbf F\)Active refutation
nonzerononzero\(\mathbf B\)Slot-level glut

Candidate-local conflict means one candidate has both channels nonzero. Slot-level glut means some pro and some con evidence exist anywhere in the slot, possibly on different candidates. Ambiguity means at least two candidates have nonzero pro support. These are distinct conditions.

$$\operatorname{card}(t)=\#\{a\mid\operatorname{pro}(t,a)\ne0\}.$$

The signable value is defined only when cardinality is one and the total con channel is zero.

4 · Rendered evidential bilattice

For \(Ev_L=L\times L\), define:

OperationDefinition
Knowledge meet\((a^+,a^-)\otimes_k(b^+,b^-)=(a^+\wedge b^+,a^-\wedge b^-)\)
Knowledge join\((a^+,a^-)\oplus_k(b^+,b^-)=(a^+\vee b^+,a^-\vee b^-)\)
Truth meet\((a^+,a^-)\wedge_t(b^+,b^-)=(a^+\wedge b^+,a^-\vee b^-)\)
Truth join\((a^+,a^-)\vee_t(b^+,b^-)=(a^+\vee b^+,a^-\wedge b^-)\)
Negation\(\neg(a^+,a^-)=(a^-,a^+)\)

The crisp corners are \(\mathbf N=(\bot,\bot)\), \(\mathbf T=(\top,\bot)\), \(\mathbf F=(\bot,\top)\), and \(\mathbf B=(\top,\top)\). Full bilattice identities, interlacing, distributivity, and equations such as \(\mathbf N\otimes_kx=\mathbf N\) and \(\mathbf F\wedge_tx=\mathbf F\) hold at this rendered layer.

Scope. Those are literal lattice equations on \(Ev_L\). They are not automatically equations of candidate maps over \(\mathbb N[X]\).

5 · Operations on testimonies

5.1 · Corroboration

$$(t\boxplus u)(a)=(\operatorname{pro}(t,a)+\operatorname{pro}(u,a),\operatorname{con}(t,a)+\operatorname{con}(u,a)).$$

Corroboration preserves rival candidates and forms a commutative monoid with identity \(\varepsilon\). It is not generally idempotent.

5.2 · Required derivation

For total \(f:A\times B\to C\), candidate pairs contribute joint provenance to \(f(a,b)\). If either candidate set is empty, there is no pair and the result is \(\varepsilon\). This is the candidate-level fail-closed rule.

5.3 · Truth-oriented derivation

A truth-oriented conjunction can target rendered \(\wedge_t\), but a candidate-preserving lift must state candidate-formation side conditions. At candidate level, \(\mathbf F\) combined with a gap has no candidate pair and yields \(\mathbf N\), although rendered \(\mathbf F\wedge_t\mathbf N=\mathbf F\). The testimony operation is therefore not a homomorphic implementation of every truth-lattice law.

5.4 · Refutation

$$(\operatorname{refute}t)(a)=(\operatorname{con}(t,a),\operatorname{pro}(t,a)).$$

Refutation is involutive.

5.5 · Strike

$$(\operatorname{strike}t)(a)=(0,\operatorname{pro}(t,a)+\operatorname{con}(t,a)).$$

Strike is idempotent and models whole-slot deletion while retaining an audit trace.

5.6 · Supersession

$$\operatorname{supersede}(t,g)=(\operatorname{strike}t,g).$$

The pair stores the struck prior separately from the fresh operative testimony. Supersession uses strike, not negation. Negating an already-refuted prior would incorrectly convert its con evidence into pro evidence.

5.7 · Token withdrawal

\(t\setminus\ell\) removes every monomial containing lineage token \(\ell\) from both channels and renormalizes. Unlike whole-slot strike, it leaves no con residue; independent derivations survive.

7 · Rendering and two corner reads

$$\operatorname{render}_\nu(t)=(\widehat\nu(\operatorname{provPro}(t)),\widehat\nu(\operatorname{provCon}(t))).$$

A homomorphism preserves zero but need not reflect it. A nonzero provenance may evaluate to bottom. Thus the structural corner and rendered corner coincide only when the valuation is zero-reflecting on the relevant image.

Normative policy. The gate uses the structural corner for conflict exclusion and a rendered pro grade for thresholding. Even zero-trust con evidence remains represented and blocks.

8 · Verification and signing gate

The gate accepts unique value \(a\) exactly when:

$$\operatorname{corner}_s(t)=\mathbf T\ \land\ \operatorname{card}(t)=1\ \land\ \widehat\nu(\operatorname{provPro}(t))\ge\theta\ \land\ \operatorname{verify}_\Gamma(t)\ \land\ a=\operatorname{value}(t).$$

Otherwise it blocks. Default routes distinguish malformed, gap, false, glut, ambiguous, low-grade, and unverified states.

9 · Implementation contract

A conforming implementation must enforce authenticated and uniquely issued lineage, normalized candidate maps, computed rather than self-asserted grades, strike(prior) in supersession, authorization for withdrawals, serialization of chronological events, and auditable verifier/gate decisions.

Implementation conformance is separate. Algebra–calculus adequacy does not prove a Scala, Go, database, or workflow implementation correct. A codebase needs an explicit refinement bridge supported by tests, mechanization, verified extraction, or equivalent evidence.

10 · Limits and non-claims

  • Verification does not prove universal semantic truth.
  • Grades are not automatically calibrated probabilities.
  • Shared upstream causes can inflate evidence unless correlation is represented.
  • Distributed supersession and withdrawal require ordering.
  • No theorem establishes superiority over every fully fair structured control on binary metrics.
  • The formal value is uniform representation, composition, auditability, and explicit conflict routing.

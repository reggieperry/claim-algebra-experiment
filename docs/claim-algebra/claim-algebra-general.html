The claim algebra — the general, updated form

A verifiable inter-agent claim as a bilattice-graded object over a provenance semiring

Visual companion to claim-algebra.html. Reflects the generalized design: the candidate carrier (G1), the render-multiplicity rule (G2), kinded citations (G3), and the parametric form lifted off the credit application. Diagrams are hand-drawn; the formal statements and proofs live in the foundations document.

The algebra carries a value through a network of agents — each typically an LLM — so that the value never loses what its sources say for and against it. A claim records its evidence on two channels, distinguishes missing from conflicting from refuted, composes lawfully, attributes blame, renders its confidence under any trust model, and refuses to sign unless it is verified, unambiguous, and over threshold. This note shows the updated structure in pictures.

1 · The four corners

Every claim sits at one of four corners, read from whether each evidence channel is empty — never from a stored number, so the corner cannot drift from its lineage. The corners live in a bilattice: two orders at once. The knowledge order asks how much is known (gap at the bottom, glut at the top); the truth order asks which way the evidence leans (false at the left, true at the right).

The bilattice is the twist of an ordinary lattice with itself: a value is a pair (a⁺, a⁻) of a degree-for and a degree-against, and negation simply swaps the two coordinates. That twist is what lets one universal construction — the provenance semiring below — supply both channels at once.

2 · The carrier updated · G1

A claim is a Testimony: a payload together with the provenance behind it, for and against. Previously the payload was a single Option[A] — one value. That quietly failed exactly where it mattered most: at a glut, two rival figures collide, and keeping one threw the other away. The updated carrier makes the payload a candidate map — each rival value kept as a key, carrying its own provenance polynomial on each channel.

Why this is more than tidiness. The old single value let the network sign one of two disagreeing corroborated figures — a confidently-wrong output. The candidate carrier blocks that structurally: two rivals read as ambiguous, and an ambiguous claim cannot clear the gate. The fix to the payload is also a fix to correctness.

3 · Provenance and the rendered grade updated · G2

Provenance is not a set of sources — it is a polynomial. Alternative derivations add (+); jointly-used supports multiply (·). These polynomials form ℕ[X], the free commutative semiring on the citation tokens X. The grade is never stored: it is the polynomial evaluated by the unique homomorphism ν̂ into whatever trust carrier you choose. Compute the lineage once; read it under any model.

4 · The four combiners

Nodes combine claims with four operations. Each acts on the two provenance channels by a semiring operation; the choice of operation per channel is what gives each combiner its distinct meaning. The asymmetry is the point — sharing one operation across both channels would collapse the distinctions.

combinerfor-channelagainst-channelmeaning & corner effect
derive  ⊗ₖ · (joint)· (joint) conjunction. A missing input annihilates the result — fail-closed: a gap sinks to a gap. essential
corroborate  ⊕ₖ + (alternative)+ (alternative) pooling sources. Agreement reinforces; a present disagreement climbs to a glut / ambiguity. Now a commutative monoid on the full carrier.
truthMeet  ∧ₜ · (joint)cross-term the refutation fork. An actively-refuted conjunct is driven to F, distinct from a missing one's N. fork is essential
refute  ¬ swap the two channels negation / retraction. Involutive; turns T into F and back, fixes N and B.
An update worth naming. Under the candidate carrier, truthMeet's against-channel is no longer a plain +; it is a three-term cross-product. The outcome that matters — a refuted conjunct lands on F, not N — is preserved exactly. So the relaxation table records the refutation fork as essential, while the specific against-channel operation that realizes it is not: it must carry the refutation, and more than one operation can.

Supersession (an amendment striking a prior clause) is built from these: it yields two claims, the struck clause refuted to F with its support kept for audit, and the operative amendment as a fresh T — never one mutated claim, which would read as a false glut.

5 · The gate and the verification axis

A value becomes a signature at exactly one place. The gate signs only a claim that is true, unambiguous, sufficiently supported, and verified. Every other outcome is a block with a reason — and crucially, none of those blocks is a confident error. Abstaining on a gap, refusing a glut, reporting a refutation: all are passes, not misses. That asymmetry is what the headline reliability metric rests on.

6 · Kinded citations updated · G3

Not every conflict is the same kind of conflict. Two sources that disagree because one was misread is an extraction error — a data check. Two defensible readings of a defined term that legitimately differ is a definitional disagreement — it escalates to a different person. A withdrawn restatement is a temporal retraction. The updated citation token carries a kind label, so the glut a downstream agent receives says not just “these sources conflict” but “they conflict this way.”

Formally the kind lifts ℕ[X] to a sorted free semiring, graded by a small join-semilattice of kinds; the grading rides along the against-channel and is provably orthogonal to the structural corner — adding it changes no existing law, it only routes.

7 · The general form

None of the above is about credit. The credit application is one instantiation; everything domain-specific is a parameter the core is written against.

8 · What changed, and what cannot

The feedback loop between this document and the code rests on knowing which properties are essential — relax one and the algebra stops being consistent — and which are free to vary.

The mathematics here is classical — Belnap's four values, Ginsberg and Fitting's bilattices, the Green–Karvounarakis–Tannen provenance semiring. The contribution is the assembly: a verifiable-claim wire format for networks of language models, with the candidate carrier, the fail-closed gate, and the kinded, two-channel provenance that keep a confidently-wrong output from ever being signed. The accompanying foundations document states each property as a theorem with its proof and its place in the relaxation table.

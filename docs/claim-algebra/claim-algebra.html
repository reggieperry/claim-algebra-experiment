A claim algebra for agent networks

The verifiable inter-agent message as a bilattice-graded object—Belnap's four values realized over the free provenance semiring, with gap and glut as distinct values, negation first-class, and verification kept as a third axis.
Working note · companion to the focused-harness actor-model design and the Credit & Loan Agreement Workbench · math via MathJax, diagrams via Mermaid + SVG

A network of LLM agents is only as trustworthy as the messages they pass each other. Today those messages are unverifiable prose, so a confident error at one hop propagates with full authority to the next; and, just as corrosively, a contradiction between two upstream agents arrives looking like a genuine absence of information. This note proposes the fix as a single mathematical object: a claim that carries its own provenance, a computed confidence, a fail-closed semantics, and a faithful distinction between not knowing and being told both. The object is best understood as a three-level tower—a categorical abstraction, realized by concrete algebraic structures, implemented as a programming type.

The one-paragraph thesis. Make every inter-agent message a value annotated by an element of a distributive bilattice: Belnap's FOUR, graded, realized as two evidential channels (support-for and support-against) over the free provenance semiring. UNRESOLVED is the knowledge-bottom, the gap (Belnap's \(\mathbf{N}\)); a second value, GLUT (Belnap's \(\mathbf{B}\)), is the knowledge-top, reached when support and refutation meet. Fail-closed is the knowledge-meet's annihilator, \(\mathbf{N}\otimes_k x=\mathbf{N}\). Pluggable trust models are homomorphisms, one per channel, via the twist representation \(\mathsf{FOUR}\cong\mathsf{TWO}\odot\mathsf{TWO}\), which evaluates a pro-polynomial and a con-polynomial separately. Negation is first-class. The message is a bilattice-graded applicative refined by a decidable verification predicate, with verification kept as a third axis distinct from Belnap's two. The mathematics (Belnap 1977; Dunn; Ginsberg–Fitting bilattices; Green–Karvounarakis–Tannen provenance; subjective logic) is decades old; using a graded bilattice as the typed wire format between LLM agents is the new part.

1 · The idea

The remedy is to stop passing values and start passing claims. Where a prose message carries only a value, a claim carries that value together with everything a downstream agent needs to trust or distrust it: where it came from, how strongly it is supported, whether anything refutes it, and whether it reproduces against its sources. The rest of this note gives that object a precise mathematical home, and §6 works it end to end on a concrete credit-deal analysis.

Ignorance is not contradiction. Two of the seven entries below, gap ≠ glut and negation, exist because a single "no trustworthy value" state is not enough. "No one told me" and "I was told two contradictory things" are different epistemic states: the first calls for a search, the second for reconciliation or escalation. A scheme with one bottom value collapses them into one, and an agent that cannot tell them apart cannot act correctly. Belnap's logic separates them natively: the gap \(\mathbf{N}\) (told nothing) from the glut \(\mathbf{B}\) (told both), and that separation is the spine of the object built here.

2 · The tower: abstraction, realization, implementation

The natural question, and the one that prompted this note, is whether the right description is category-theoretic. The answer is yes, with discipline: category theory belongs at the top, where it captures the abstraction several concrete structures share, and it should be used only where it earns its keep. Below it sit the algebraic structures that realize the abstraction, and below those, the type that implements them. The middle floor is a bilattice (two interlaced lattices rather than one), and because of that the top level does its work a little differently.

Where category theory does real work. Category theory belongs at the top in exactly three ways: (i) the universal property of the free semiring, applied per channel, which keeps "compute provenance once, evaluate many trust models" a one-line theorem; (ii) preservation of the zeros by morphisms, which makes fail-closed hold under every trust model at once; and (iii) the twist (product) representation of distributive bilattices, \(\mathsf{FOUR}\cong\mathsf{TWO}\odot\mathsf{TWO}\), which lets (i) and (ii) survive the move from a semiring to a bilattice. The twist says a bilattice is just a pair of lattice computations bolted together, so the semiring machinery applies twice rather than not at all. Past these three, further categorical generality would be decoration, and the note drops to the algebra. Abstraction is a tool for compression, not a display of generality.

Two layers, two names. The general object at the algebraic and type levels (a value, or UNRESOLVED, carried with its provenance and a grade in the value space \(K\)) is a Testimony: a value held together with what its sources attest for and against it. It is value-agnostic: the payload may be a fact, a cost, or a trust level, and the entire algebra of §3 and §4 operates at this layer. A Claim is a Testimony refined by a verification predicate, a Testimony that asserts something and can be checked against its provenance. So a Claim is a Testimony that verifies. The framework keeps the name "claim algebra" because verification is its motivating purpose, but the general structure underneath is a Testimony. What distinguishes this object from a plain graded value is what \(K\) is: not a commutative semiring but a distributive bilattice, so a Testimony carries a Belnap-valued (graded) annotation rather than a single semiring grade. The cost-routing instance (§8) is naturally a Testimony, a graded cost; the workbench's cited facts and Diplomacy's promises are Claims, where verification and the for/against bookkeeping do the work.

3 · The categorical layer abstraction

3.1 · Claims compose—so they live in a monoidal category

Agents take claims in and put claims out; they are morphisms. Two claims can be combined into one (two extracted figures combined into a derived one). The structure that captures "objects, morphisms, and a way to combine things side by side" is a symmetric monoidal category \((\mathcal{C}, \otimes, I)\): the tensor \(\otimes\) is conjunctive composition, and the unit \(I\) is the trivially-true claim. An agent network is then a string diagram in \(\mathcal{C}\)—boxes are agents, wires carry claims, side-by-side wires are conjunction, and the diagram's value is read by composing the boxes [3, 4, 5, 6].

One subtlety the bilattice forces here. Once values can be refuted, "conjunction" is no longer a single operation: there are two monoidal products sharing the same objects, the knowledge-tensor and the truth-tensor (§4.3). The string diagram is unchanged as a picture; what changes is that each box must say which tensor its fan-in uses. Fail-closed pins the default to the knowledge-tensor.

3.2 · The trust annotation is a lax monoidal functor

A claim is a value decorated with provenance and confidence. The decoration is a functor \(F\) that knows how to combine decorations when values combine. The precise statement is that \(F\) is a lax monoidal functor: there is a natural map $$ F a \;\otimes\; F b \;\longrightarrow\; F(a \otimes b), $$ which says "given a decorated \(a\) and a decorated \(b\), produce a decorated \(a \otimes b\)": combine the two annotations into the annotation of the combination. This is not a coincidence of vocabulary: applicative functors are exactly lax monoidal functors [2, 7]. Because the decoration is now a pair of channels, the lax map combines pro with pro and con with con; §5's type writes this out in a language with a compiler.

3.3 · The universal property—compute provenance once, evaluate many trust models

Here is where category theory does concrete work, and where moving to a bilattice could most easily have broken it. Let \(X\) be the set of leaf sources (this clause, that tool output). The provenance of a derived claim is a polynomial in the \(X\): a sum (alternative derivations) of products (conjoined supports). These polynomials form the free commutative semiring \(\mathbb{N}[X]\), the free object on \(X\) in commutative semirings. Freeness is a universal property: for any commutative semiring \(K\) and any valuation \(\nu : X \to K\), there is a unique semiring homomorphism \(\hat\nu : \mathbb{N}[X] \to K\) with \(\hat\nu \circ \iota = \nu\). You compute the lineage polynomial once and every trust measure is a homomorphic evaluation of it, the Green–Karvounarakis–Tannen provenance-semiring theorem [1].

The obstacle. The GKT theorem is about a semiring. A bilattice is not a semiring; it is two lattices interlaced (two semirings' worth of structure, plus a negation tying them). A naive reading fears the compute-once-evaluate-many guarantee is lost: there is no single free bilattice homomorphism that the GKT result hands you.

The rescue is the twist. A distributive bilattice is, up to isomorphism, a product of two copies of an underlying lattice with a swap: \(\mathsf{FOUR}\cong\mathsf{TWO}\odot\mathsf{TWO}\), and more generally \(K\cong L\odot L\) for a lattice \(L\) [11]. Read an element as a pair \((a^{+},a^{-})\): degree-for and degree-against, the evidence-for / evidence-against reading of Dunn [10]. Then a derived claim carries two independent lineage polynomials, a pro-polynomial \(p^{+}\in\mathbb{N}[X]\) and a con-polynomial \(p^{-}\in\mathbb{N}[X]\); each is evaluated by its own GKT homomorphism into the underlying lattice \(L\); and the bilattice value is read off the resulting pair. So the universal property is not lost: it applies twice, once per channel. "Compute once, evaluate many" becomes "compute two lineages once, evaluate each under one homomorphism," and a glut is simply the case where both evaluated channels are non-bottom. Because each \(\hat h\) is a homomorphism, evaluating the trust model and running the network commute: you can swap the trust model without recomputing the network, re-evaluating the same two lineage polynomials under a different \(\hat h\).

Why fail-closed is free, and now precisely which zero. A bilattice has two bottoms: the knowledge-bottom \(\mathbf{N}\) (the gap) and the truth-bottom \(\mathbf{F}\) (refuted). Every lattice homomorphism on each channel preserves its bottom, so both survive evaluation into every trust model. Fail-closed is the law of the knowledge-meet: a conjunctive derivation with a missing (gap) input lands on \(\mathbf{N}\), under every \(\hat h\). A refuted input, by contrast, drives the truth-meet to \(\mathbf{F}\): the result is false, not unknown. The two outcomes differ, and §4.3 makes the fork explicit. Fail-closed is therefore not a feature you implement once and hope every agent respects; it is a structural property of the category of lattices, holding channel-wise.

4 · The algebraic layer realization

4.1 · The object: a distributive bilattice over an absorbing-zero lattice

A distributive bilattice \((K,\le_t,\le_k)\) is a set carrying two lattice orderings at once, a truth order \(\le_t\) and a knowledge (information) order \(\le_k\), that interlace (each lattice operation is monotone with respect to the other order), together with a negation \(\lnot\) that inverts \(\le_t\) and preserves \(\le_k\). The smallest nontrivial one is Belnap's \(\mathsf{FOUR}\) [9]. The single safety law of a one-channel semiring, \(0\otimes x=0\), survives inside it; the bilattice adds the missing structure around that law.

The clean way to hold it is the twist: \(K\cong L\odot L\), an element being a pair \((a^{+},a^{-})\) of degree-for and degree-against drawn from an underlying lattice \(L\). All four operations are then componentwise min/max, and negation is the swap:

OperationOn pairs \((a^{+},a^{-})\)IdentityAnnihilatorReading
truth-meet \(\wedge_t\)\((\min a^{+},\ \max a^{-})\)\(\mathbf{T}=(\top,\bot)\)\(\mathbf{F}=(\bot,\top)\)conservative AND on truth: refutation wins
truth-join \(\vee_t\)\((\max a^{+},\ \min a^{-})\)\(\mathbf{F}\)\(\mathbf{T}\)optimistic OR on truth: support wins
knowledge-meet \(\otimes_k\)\((\min a^{+},\ \min a^{-})\)\(\mathbf{B}=(\top,\top)\)\(\mathbf{N}=(\bot,\bot)\)fail-closed: a gap input erases evidence
knowledge-join \(\oplus_k\)\((\max a^{+},\ \max a^{-})\)\(\mathbf{N}\)\(\mathbf{B}\)corroboration: pool evidence; conflict → glut
negation \(\lnot\)\((a^{-},\ a^{+})\)involution: \(\lnot\lnot=\mathrm{id}\)swap for and against
Why the knowledge-meet is the default conjunction. A purely positive confidence scheme has only a "for" channel, so it can only ever compute in the knowledge lattice: conjunction is the knowledge-meet \(\otimes_k\) (annihilator \(\mathbf{N}\)) and corroboration is the knowledge-join \(\oplus_k\) (identity \(\mathbf{N}\)). What such a scheme can never reach is the top \(\mathbf{B}\), because with nothing on the "against" channel no value can be pushed to \((\,\cdot\,,\top)\). Add the "against" channel and the rest of the bilattice (the value \(\mathbf{F}\), the value \(\mathbf{B}\), the truth lattice, negation) appears for free. So fail-closed conjunction stays exactly what it was, the knowledge-meet; the bilattice simply makes the other three operations expressible.

4.2 · The universal instance and the concrete instances

The universal \(K\) is the twist over the provenance polynomial semiring, \(\mathbb{N}[X]\odot\mathbb{N}[X]\): you compute the two lineages in it once (§3.3). The concrete \(K\) you evaluate into is a design choice. The smallest interesting one is \(\mathsf{FOUR}\) itself, the deterministic True / False / Unknown / Conflicted signal. The graded one is \([0,1]^2\): a degree-for and a degree-against. Jøsang's subjective logic [12] embeds in it as the consistent simplex \(a^{+}+a^{-}\le 1\) (belief = for, disbelief = against, uncertainty \(=1-a^{+}-a^{-}\) measuring gap-ness toward \(\mathbf{N}\)). The glut region \(a^{+}+a^{-}>1\), culminating in \(\mathbf{B}=(\top,\top)\), lies outside that simplex, so the bilattice extends subjective logic with a conflict corner a single opinion cannot express; on a binary frame it relates likewise to Dempster–Shafer belief functions [13]. The standard one-channel evaluation semirings survive as the per-channel \(L\) you twist: the Viterbi lattice \(([0,1], \max, \times)\) for most-likely-derivation confidence, the fuzzy lattice \(([0,1], \max, \min)\) for weakest-link, the tropical semiring \((\mathbb{R}\cup\{\infty\}, \min, +)\) for cost-to-verify, and the Boolean lattice for bare support [18].

The graded confidence chain is the truth axis \(L\), not the bilattice. The four-element chain \(\{\bot<\mathrm{Low}<\mathrm{Med}<\mathrm{High}\}\) (a bounded distributive lattice, hence an idempotent commutative semiring) is the underlying lattice \(L\) of the twist; it supplies the graded truth axis of Figure 4. The four-element chain and the four-element bilattice \(\mathsf{FOUR}\) are different objects of the same size: the chain is totally ordered (one axis), \(\mathsf{FOUR}\) is the two-axis diamond. Reading the High/Med/Low signal as the whole structure is the conflation to avoid; it is one axis of two.

4.3 · Fail-closed semantics—a fork, deliberately resolved

"Fail-closed" is a principle borrowed from security and safety engineering: when a result is missing, unverifiable, or uncertain, the system defaults to the safe, restrictive state (no usable value) rather than proceeding with a guess. The metaphor is a gate. Fail-closed: on failure the gate shuts and nothing passes. Fail-open: on failure the gate opens and everything passes. A lock that fails closed stays locked when the power dies; a firewall that fails closed denies all traffic when its rules crash; default-deny authorization is fail-closed. The two choices have opposite failure modes, which is the whole reason to pick one. A fail-closed system's mistakes are over-refusals: it returns nothing when it could have answered, a loss of throughput, not of correctness. A fail-open system's mistakes are false accepts: it lets a wrong value through with authority, a loss of correctness. The deliberate asymmetry is what makes the choice safe: the system is allowed to return UNRESOLVED and is never penalized for it, and is penalized only for a confident value that turns out wrong. The cost of uncertainty is paid as "no answer, escalate to a human," never as a wrong answer that reaches a signature. Most language-model systems fail open: uncertain, they emit a fluent guess, and flipping that default is the point.

With \(\mathbf{F}\) and \(\mathbf{B}\) present there are two meets, and they disagree on what a bad input does:

Inputsknowledge-meet \(\otimes_k\)truth-meet \(\wedge_t\)Gloss
\(\mathbf{T}\) and \(\mathbf{N}\) (a gap)\(\mathbf{N}\)\(\mathbf{N}\)missing support ⇒ unknown either way (both meets agree)
\(\mathbf{T}\) and \(\mathbf{F}\) (refuted)\(\mathbf{N}\)—"we now know nothing"\(\mathbf{F}\)—"the result is false"they diverge: this is the choice the bilattice exposes

The derivation is direct: \(\mathbf{T}\otimes_k\mathbf{F}=(\top,\bot)\otimes_k(\bot,\top)=(\bot,\bot)=\mathbf{N}\), whereas \(\mathbf{T}\wedge_t\mathbf{F}=(\top,\bot)\wedge_t(\bot,\top)=(\bot,\top)=\mathbf{F}\). The fail-closed default for derivation is the knowledge-meet, so missing support propagates closed to \(\mathbf{N}\) across every hop and (since channel homomorphisms preserve the bottom) under every trust model. What the second channel adds is that a genuine refutation of a conjunct can now be expressed as \(\mathbf{F}\) under the truth-meet. The bilattice does not pick the meet for you; the engineering decision is to declare the knowledge-meet for "missing support" and reserve the truth-meet for "actively refuted."

4.4 · The evidential pair is Belnap's second axis—verification is a third

Three questions hide behind a single confident sentence, and they are independent. Confidence answers "how strong is the derivation, assuming the inputs hold?", and that grading lives on the truth axis of Figure 4 (the \(L\) of the twist). The evidential content (supported, refuted, neither, both) is the for/against pair, and that pair is exactly Belnap's structure: the knowledge order runs gap \(\mathbf{N}\) → glut \(\mathbf{B}\), the truth order runs \(\mathbf{F}\) → \(\mathbf{T}\), both internal to the evidential pair. Verification answers a different question again: does the value actually reproduce against the provenance it cites? Re-resolve the citation, re-match the quote. This is a decidable predicate \(\mathrm{verify} : \mathrm{Claim}\,a \to \mathrm{Bool}\), and it is not one of Belnap's two orderings. It is a third axis.

A confusion worth naming. It is tempting to draw a 2×2 grid with confidence on one axis and verification on the other and call it "a bilattice in the sense of Belnap." It is not. Belnap's two orders are truth (false → true) and knowledge (gap → glut), and both are evidential. A faithful object therefore tracks three things, not two: support-for, support-against (these two are the Belnap value), and verified? (the orthogonal refinement). "High confidence · unverified" is a point on the verification axis crossed with a high truth-grade; it is not the glut \(\mathbf{B}\). The glut is "supported and refuted," a different idea entirely.

The acceptance policy that gates a signature is then the conjunction: the value must be True and unambiguous — a single candidate, not gap, not glut, not false, not a field of rivals — its derivation grade must clear a threshold, and it must verify: $$ \mathrm{accept}(c)\iff \mathrm{corner}(c)=\mathbf{T}\ \wedge\ \mathrm{cardinality}(c)=1\ \wedge\ \mathrm{grade}(c)\ge\theta\ \wedge\ \mathrm{verify}(c). $$ Current agent swarms live in the dangerous cell: fluent and confident, unchecked. The algebra propagates the evidential pair faithfully; the verification axis is what verify() establishes; and only "True · unambiguous · cleared · verified" should reach a signature.

5 · The type layer code

The categorical statement of §3.2 (applicative = lax monoidal functor) is a programming type. The grade is not a stored channel but a bilattice element rendered from the two provenance polynomials by the homomorphism ν̂ (§3.3), so there is no second source of truth to drift. The two names of §2 appear directly: Testimony[A] is the graded value the algebra acts on—its grade rendered on demand, not carried—and Claim[A] is a Testimony refined by verification. Here it is in Scala, lightly cleaned; the invariant-bearing scalars are distinct (or opaque) types behind fail-closed constructors—illegal states unrepresentable, with the residual invariants carried by law and property tests.

// The underlying lattice L of the twist (the per-channel confidence). A DISTINCT
// sealed type, not an opaque alias of Double — the only constructors are bottom, top,
// and the clamping fail-closed deg, so the (0,1] invariant holds AND a derived instance
// can't self-loop (an opaque `= Double` would resolve Order[Double] back to its own given).
sealed trait Lev
object Lev:
  case object Bot extends Lev                          // ⊥  no evidence on this channel
  private final case class Deg(p: Double) extends Lev // p ∈ (0,1]; private — only `deg` builds it
  val bottom: Lev = Bot
  val top: Lev    = Deg(1.0)
  def deg(p: Double): Lev =                            // the only constructor; fail-closed
    if !(p > 0.0) then Bot else if p >= 1.0 then top else Deg(p)   // NaN and p ≤ 0 ⇒ ⊥
  extension (x: Lev)
    def strength: Double  = x match { case Bot => 0.0; case Deg(p) => p }
    def isBottom: Boolean = x match { case Bot => true; case Deg(_) => false }
  def meet(a: Lev, b: Lev): Lev = if a.strength <= b.strength then a else b  // min (weaker)
  def join(a: Lev, b: Lev): Lev = if a.strength >= b.strength then a else b  // max (stronger)
  given Order[Lev] = Order.fromLessThan((a, b) => a.strength < b.strength)

// THE RENDERED bilattice element: an evidential PAIR (for, against)—the twist L ⊙ L. NOT stored on a
// Testimony; it is the homomorphic image ν̂(pro), ν̂(con) of the two provenance polynomials under a
// chosen trust model L (see Testimony.renderEv). The four operations are the bilattice it lives in.
final case class Ev(pro: Lev, con: Lev)              // (a⁺, a⁻)
object Ev:
  val N = Ev(Lev.bottom, Lev.bottom)                 // gap—knowledge-bottom, the UNRESOLVED value
  val T = Ev(Lev.top,    Lev.bottom)                 // true—supported, unrefuted
  val F = Ev(Lev.bottom, Lev.top)                    // false—refuted, unsupported
  val B = Ev(Lev.top,    Lev.top)                    // glut—knowledge-top, told both

  // FOUR OPERATIONS, all componentwise (see §4.1 table):
  def kmeet(a: Ev, b: Ev) = Ev(Lev.meet(a.pro, b.pro), Lev.meet(a.con, b.con)) // ⊗ₖ FAIL-CLOSED: N absorbs
  def kjoin(a: Ev, b: Ev) = Ev(Lev.join(a.pro, b.pro), Lev.join(a.con, b.con)) // ⊕ₖ corroborate; conflict → B
  def tmeet(a: Ev, b: Ev) = Ev(Lev.meet(a.pro, b.pro), Lev.join(a.con, b.con)) // ∧ₜ truth-AND; F absorbs
  def tjoin(a: Ev, b: Ev) = Ev(Lev.join(a.pro, b.pro), Lev.meet(a.con, b.con)) // ∨ₜ truth-OR
  def negate(a: Ev): Ev   = Ev(a.con, a.pro)                                   // ¬ swap channels

  enum Belnap { case Gap, True, False, Glut }
  def corner(a: Ev): Belnap = (a.pro.isBottom, a.con.isBottom) match  // read off the pair
    case (true, true)   => Belnap.Gap
    case (false, true)  => Belnap.True
    case (true, false)  => Belnap.False
    case (false, false) => Belnap.Glut               // both channels fire—the contradiction value

// Provenance is the free commutative semiring ℕ[X] (§4): a polynomial over lineage tokens, carried
// TWICE (a pro-lineage and a con-lineage). + is ALTERNATIVE derivation (corroboration), · is JOINT

// THE TESTIMONY: a value with its two provenance polynomials. The grade is NOT a field — it is
// rendered from the polynomials (renderEv), and the Belnap corner is read STRUCTURALLY from whether
// each channel is empty, so no stored grade can drift from its provenance.
final case class Testimony[+A](value: Option[A], provPro: Prov, provCon: Prov)
// READABLE SKETCH: this single-value form is shown for clarity; the SHIPPED carrier generalizes
// `value: Option[A]` to a candidate map `A →₀ Channels(pro, con)`, so corroboration is a commutative
// monoid that keeps disagreeing rivals as DISTINCT candidates (an ambiguity, cardinality ≥ 2) —
// not the left-biased `orElse` below — and truthMeet's con-channel is the three-term cross-term
// ca·pb + pa·cb + ca·cb (recovering F ∧ₜ F = F). See claim-algebra-general.html and the foundations.

object Testimony:
  def corner(t: Testimony[?]): Belnap =                       // model-free, off the two polynomials
    Belnap.from(t.provPro.isZero, t.provCon.isZero)

  // ⊗ₖ derive — fail-closed by the ANNIHILATOR: BOTH channels combine by JOINT use ·, so a gap (0)
  // sinks the pro-product to 0 ⇒ no value, under every trust model. (§4.3 default for "missing".)
  def derive[A, B, C](a: Testimony[A], b: Testimony[B])(f: (A, B) => C): Testimony[C] =
    val pro = Prov.times(a.provPro, b.provPro)
    val v   = if pro.isZero then None else (a.value, b.value).mapN(f)
    Testimony(v, pro, Prov.times(a.provCon, b.provCon))

  // ∧ₜ truth-meet — the F7 fork: pro by ·, con by +, so an actively-REFUTED conjunct goes to F, not
  // the gap N that ⊗ₖ gives a merely-missing one. Same skeleton as derive; the con-channel uses +.
  def truthMeet[A, B, C](a: Testimony[A], b: Testimony[B])(f: (A, B) => C): Testimony[C] =
    val pro = Prov.times(a.provPro, b.provPro)
    val v   = if pro.isZero then None else (a.value, b.value).mapN(f)
    Testimony(v, pro, Prov.plus(a.provCon, b.provCon))

  // ⊕ₖ corroborate — both channels by ALTERNATIVE +: agreement pools; a support meeting a refutation
  // makes both channels non-empty ⇒ the glut B.
  def corroborate[A](a: Testimony[A], b: Testimony[A]): Testimony[A] =
    Testimony(a.value.orElse(b.value),
              Prov.plus(a.provPro, b.provPro), Prov.plus(a.provCon, b.provCon))

  // ¬ refute — swap the channels. F5 supersession is refute(old) [struck → F, kept on the record]
  // paired with a fresh T [the operative amendment]: two claims, never one (a clobber reads as a glut).
  def refute[A](a: Testimony[A]): Testimony[A] = Testimony(a.value, a.provCon, a.provPro)

  // THE grade, rendered: ν̂ on each channel into the bilattice, under a chosen trust model L (here Lev,
  // the fuzzy carrier). Swap L (Viterbi, counting, Boolean) without rerunning the network (§3.3).
  def renderEv[A](t: Testimony[A])(nu: Lineage => Lev): Ev =
    Ev(t.provPro.evaluate(nu), t.provCon.evaluate(nu))

// A CLAIM is a Testimony refined by verification—opaque, so the only way to build one is to run the
// verifier. (Earlier drafts wrote this as `Refined VerifiesAgainstProvenance`; because the predicate
// needs the corpus, it is a runtime smart constructor returning Option, not a pure refinement.)
opaque type Claim[+A] = Testimony[A]
object Claim:
  def verify[A](t: Testimony[A], v: Testimony[A] => Boolean): Option[Claim[A]] = Option.when(v(t))(t)
  extension [A](c: Claim[A]) def testimony: Testimony[A] = c
Why an applicative, not a monad. Monadic >>= lets later structure depend on an earlier value and short-circuits on the first failure, discarding the lineage of the other conjuncts. The Validation applicative is deliberately not a monad, precisely so it accumulates [2]. Here it must accumulate both the pro- and con-lineages of every conjunct, because a downstream agent needs the full two-channel provenance for verification, blame-assignment, and to tell a glut from a gap. The "graded" qualifier means the \((\mathrm{Prov},\mathrm{Prov},\mathrm{Ev})\) annotation is an effect grade produced by composition, never written by the payload [8]. The single legitimately-monadic edge (an agent choosing whom to ask next from a prior answer) remains a justified escape hatch, not the default.

The other realizations move in step. In Haskell the object is Testimony a = Writer (Prov, Prov) (Maybe a) exposed as an Applicative only (the monoid is the product of the two provenance semirings; the grade is rendered from them by ν̂, not carried), with Claim via a Liquid Haskell refinement, and negate a total function swapping the two Prov components. In the harness's Go (no higher-kinded types) the Testimony struct carries a second provenance field and the Conf enum becomes the four-corner Ev; And still pattern-matches the gap first (now Ev.N) as an explicit early return, but the glut is representable as a value rather than silently folded into the gap.

6 · A first worked example: credit deal analysis

Put the object to work on the problem that motivates it. A credit-analysis network reads a borrower's filings and produces a leverage figure for a memo a human will sign. Three agents do the front-line work: one extracts total debt from a filing, one extracts EBITDA, and a third divides them to report leverage. In a prose pipeline the third agent receives "debt is $1.2bn" and "EBITDA is $400m" as sentences, believes them, and emits "leverage is 3.0x" as another, with nothing recording where $1.2bn came from, how sure the extractor was, or what should happen if the EBITDA agent failed and guessed. An error at hop one reaches the signed memo at hop three wearing the same confident clothes as a fact. As claims, each figure instead arrives carrying its provenance and its evidential pair, and the ratio agent composes them under the knowledge-meet.

Fail-closed on a missing input. Suppose the debt extractor finds no usable figure: its claim is the gap \(\mathbf{N}\). Leverage is debt divided by EBITDA, a knowledge-meet, and \(\mathbf{N}\otimes_k x=\mathbf{N}\), so the leverage claim is itself \(\mathbf{N}\) (UNRESOLVED), no matter how confident the EBITDA extractor was. The missing input cannot be laundered into a plausible 3.0x, and the agent that owns the ratio is allowed to return "unresolved, escalate" without penalty (§4.3).

Disagreement becomes an ambiguity, not a gap. Now add a fourth agent: a second debt extractor that reads $1.5bn from a different exhibit, disagreeing with the first agent's $1.2bn. Both figures are cited, and neither denies the other. Corroborating them under \(\oplus_k\) keeps both as rival candidates — the corner stays True but the cardinality is 2, an ambiguity, which the gate blocks because no single value is signable. (A glut \(\mathbf{B}\) is the distinct case where the same value is asserted and refuted — a source that contradicts the $1.2bn, or a withdrawn restatement.) This is the case a single-bottom scheme cannot express: it would collapse "$1.2bn cited and $1.5bn cited" to the same UNRESOLVED as "no debt figure was ever found." The two states call for opposite actions (the gap a search, the ambiguity reconciliation or escalation), and the downstream agent can branch on which it received.

An amendment is a retraction, not a clobber. Say a later filing amends the debt figure, superseding the first extraction. Rather than silently overwriting it, the superseding source refutes the prior support: negation swaps the old for-evidence into the con-channel (§4.1), so the record shows that the first figure was retracted, by what, and when, instead of the earlier value simply vanishing.

The signature gate. The memo may be signed only when the leverage claim is True and unambiguous (a single candidate — not gap, not glut, not false, not a field of rivals), its grade clears the threshold, and it verifies against its cited spans: $$ \mathrm{accept}(c)\iff \mathrm{corner}(c)=\mathbf{T}\ \wedge\ \mathrm{cardinality}(c)=1\ \wedge\ \mathrm{grade}(c)\ge\theta\ \wedge\ \mathrm{verify}(c). $$ A high-grade leverage figure whose citation does not reproduce sits in the danger cell of Figure 6 and is held back; a glut is routed to reconciliation; a gap is routed to a search. The three axes (evidential value, derivation grade, verification) gate the signature independently, and all three must hold (§4.4).

Routing a glut by kind (G3). Each Lineage token carries the kind of evidence it is — an extraction, a definitional reading, a temporal retraction, an independent verification — so a glut does more than say "reconcile": the map \(\hat\kappa\) reads the kind-set off the con-channel and names which desk owns it (a misread figure to data-quality, a withdrawn restatement to the deal lead, an independent refutation to audit). Because the kind rides the provenance, \(\hat\kappa\) is an additive-monoid homomorphism that unions kinds across the whole composition for free; the routing falls out of one read with no per-kind plumbing. It is a pure decoration — never an input to the gate or the grade, so the accept decision is unchanged (a conservative extension). This is a parsimony and blame-attribution capability, not a further reduction in confidently-wrong signatures: against a fair structured control that hand-extracts the same per-kind signals, it ties (see experiment-findings.md, finding 7).

This one example exercises every moving part: the two-channel evidential value, fail-closed conjunction, gap distinct from glut, negation as retraction, and verification as the third axis. The three instances that follow take the same object to other value spaces and scales.

8 · A second instance: cost routing in an agent fulfillment network

Section 7 read the workbench as the \(N=1\) specialization. The same object at a different value space is a network of fulfillment-center agents finding the cheapest way to move an item to a customer, the cost sibling. Working it through exercises the same core moves (annihilator as fail-closed, the verification axis, the homomorphism) on a problem that looks nothing like clause extraction, and it is the natural setting for many agents rather than one. The bilattice touches it in one specific place: the confidence riding on an advertised cost becomes Belnap-valued, while the cost dimension itself stays one-dimensional.

8.1 · The tropical specialization

Take the cost channel to be the tropical (min-plus) semiring \((\mathbb{R}\cup\{+\infty\},\ \min,\ +,\ +\infty,\ 0)\). Nodes are fulfillment centers; edges are lanes (center-to-center transfer, or center-to-customer last mile); an edge's weight is its cost. Conjunction \(+\) adds cost along a path; disjunction \(\min\) picks the cheapest alternative. The tropical zero \(+\infty\) plays a dual role: as the \(\min\)-identity it is "no route found yet" (beaten by any real route), and as the \(+\)-annihilator it is "this leg is infeasible" (\(+\infty + x = +\infty\), poisoning any route through it). Optimal routing is the Kleene closure \(a^{*} = 1 \oplus a \oplus a^{2} \oplus \cdots\) of the tropical adjacency matrix, the computation Bellman–Ford and Floyd–Warshall carry out [18, 19, 20, 21]; it converges when there is no negative-cost cycle, a condition automatically met by non-negative shipping costs.

The "ship direct, or forward through a closer center then ship" decision is one \(\min\) over \(+\)-products: $$ \mathrm{cost}(A\!\to\!\mathrm{cust}) \;=\; [A\!\to\!\mathrm{cust}] \;\oplus\; \big([A\!\to\!B]\otimes[B\!\to\!\mathrm{cust}]\big) \;\oplus\; \cdots \;=\; \min\{\,9,\ 2{+}3,\ \dots\,\}, $$ so shipping direct at \(\$9\) loses to forwarding through \(B\) at \(\$2 + \$3 = \$5\). The three center "types" (distribution-only, direct-to-customer, hybrid) are not special cases in the algebra; they are a derived property of each node's incident edges (whether a center-to-customer edge is present), not a tag the math reads.

What the bilattice does and does not change here. The cost itself is a scalar in a totally ordered semiring; there is no "for/against" on a number, and the note does not bilattice-ify cost. What becomes Belnap-valued is the confidence a center attaches to its advertised cost: that cost can be actively refuted (a probe contradicts it), value \(\mathbf{F}\); this is distinct from an unprobed lane, value \(\mathbf{N}\), and from two probes that disagree, value \(\mathbf{B}\). The cost stays tropical, and a lane that is infeasible rather than mispriced is carried on the cost channel as \(+\infty\); the evidential pair rides alongside it.

8.2 · Intelligent agents make the edge weight a claim

A scalar lane cost assumes the cost is known. An intelligent center predicts it (transit time, capacity, stockout risk), so the edge weight is not a number but a graded value with provenance and an evidential pair: a Testimony[Cost]. This is the §2 distinction in action: a cost is not an assertion, so it is naturally a Testimony, not a Claim; the verifier field below, which lets the receiver re-check the lane against evidence, is what lifts it to a Claim[Cost]. The construction needs naming precisely, because it is easy to mis-state:

  • Under \(\otimes\) (compose a route) it is a genuine product: add the cost (tropical \(\otimes = {+}\)) and meet the evidential pair under the knowledge-meet \(\otimes_k\), componentwise (the weakest-link reading). A leg with no usable support, whether unprobed or refuted, forces the composed pro-channel to \(\bot\), so the route is only as confident as its weakest leg; a confirmed-down lane is carried on the cost channel as \(+\infty\), not as a false evidential pair.
  • Under \(\oplus\) (choose among routes) it is not the componentwise direct product. It is the tropical-weight-with-output construction familiar from weighted finite-state transducers [22]: the tropical cost selects the winning route, and the route, evidential pair, and provenance ride along as an output label, concatenated under \(\otimes\) and projected from the arg-min at each \(\oplus\). It is a well-defined, associative \(\oplus\) only once equal-cost ties resolve deterministically (equivalently, a lexicographic semiring with cost primary).

The route witness here is an ordered path, the free monoid under concatenation, a deliberate strengthening of §5's unordered Prov(Set). Order matters here: the loop check in §8.3 reads it.

A center \(B\) advertises to a neighbor \(A\), per destination, a cost-to-serve claim—a path-vector advertisement:

final case class ReachClaim(
  dest:     DestId,                                  // the QUESTION: which customer / region
  cost:     Cost | Unreachable,                      // tropical VALUE (Unreachable = +inf)
  route:    Vector[Leg],                             // PROVENANCE—an ORDERED path witness (free monoid)
  ev:       Ev,                                      // EVIDENTIAL pair on the advertised cost:
                                                     //   True = probe confirms the cost · False = probe refutes it
                                                     //   Gap = unprobed · Glut = probes disagree
  verifier: EvidenceRef,                             // VERIFICATION handle—re-check the lane vs evidence
  asOf:     Instant, ttl: Duration,                  // freshness (a min-lattice)
  basis:    Indicative | Reservable(CapacityToken))  // estimate vs committable hold (Boolean-AND)

\(A\) relaxes by composing its lane to \(B\) with \(B\)'s claim: cost is tropical \(+\) (with \(+\infty\) absorbing, fail-closed); route is \((A\!\to\!B)\) prepended; the evidential pair is the knowledge-meet \(\otimes_k\) of the two legs' pairs; freshness is the \(\min\); and basis is reservable only if both legs are. \(A\)'s own advertised cost is the \(\min\) over neighbors, carrying the arg-min's label and keeping the runners-up as alternative lineage. The message is thus a product of several little monoids at once (cost tropical, evidence the bilattice, route a free monoid, freshness a min-lattice, feasibility Boolean), which is why the \(\oplus\) is selective rather than componentwise. Its verifier field is the §4.4 third axis: an advertised cost is a Claim[Cost] whose verify() re-resolves the lane against the agent's evidence, so a high-grade-but-unverified quote sits in the danger cell of Figure 6, and, separately, a glut flags two rate cards that disagree.

8.3 · Provenance buys loop-freedom

Carrying the full route lineage, and discarding any advertisement whose route already contains the receiver, makes this a path-vector protocol—the AS_PATH loop check of BGP [24]. Path-vector routing is inherently loop-free and therefore has no count-to-infinity. The contrast is instructive: bare distance-vector routing (RIP) carries only a metric, so it leans on split-horizon and poison-reverse, which suppress only two-hop loops, plus a metric-as-infinity ceiling, and still suffers count-to-infinity on longer loops [25]. The same provenance field that buys auditability buys loop-freedom. And an Unreachable advertisement (\(+\infty\), the annihilator) is an active route withdrawal, distinct from a stale entry timing out: a center refusing to guess a lane it cannot confirm, and the network declining to route through it. None of this rides on the evidential pair; loop-freedom rides on the order of the provenance witness.

What this does not give you for free. "Compute the route once, then re-optimize for cost, time, or carbon by swapping the homomorphism" is false, and a practitioner will object immediately: changing the objective changes the chosen lane. The homomorphism theorem (§3.3) re-evaluates a fixed, un-collapsed lineage polynomial under many valuations, but shortest-path selection is itself a min-fold under one cost valuation, and collapsing to the cost-min discards the alternatives, including the possibly-different min-time path. What is genuinely reused is the path-enumeration structure, not the per-objective minimization; each objective still runs its own min-fold, because materializing the full un-collapsed lineage to reuse it across objectives is itself exponential. True simultaneous multi-objective routing needs a lexicographic semiring (a total order: cost, ties broken by time) or a Pareto / antichain semiring (labels are sets of non-dominated cost vectors; \(\oplus\) is non-dominated union, \(\otimes\) is vector addition), the latter computing the frontier once but at a cost that is NP-hard with a possibly exponential frontier [26]. The bilattice upgrade does not change this; it concerns the confidence channel, not the cost objective.

9 · A third instance: adversarial games (Diplomacy)

The first two instances are cooperative: centers have no incentive to lie about lane costs. The sharper test is an adversarial setting where agents do have an incentive to deceive, and Diplomacy is the canonical one [28]: seven powers move simultaneously on a map of 1901 Europe, there is no luck, all variance lives in negotiation and in predicting others' moves, and a promise is binding only if kept. It splits cleanly into a part the algebra governs and a part it does not, and the boundary sits further in than a one-channel semiring could put it, because the trust model is itself a graded bilattice value.

9.1 · The board substrate—the consistent fragment of Belnap

Board state is the cooperative substrate, and the algebra governs it directly. Unit positions, supply-center ownership, and the legality and resolution of orders are objective facts the adjudicator computes: adjudication is a pure, deterministic function \(\mathrm{Orders}\times\mathrm{Board}\to(\mathrm{Board},\mathrm{Outcomes})\), and the engine is the verification oracle. Three claim types ride the wire: a StateClaim (a verified board fact), an Order (a Hold / Move / Support / Convoy commitment, legality-verified and adjudicated), and a Deal (a negotiation message, below). No power can hallucinate the board, and an illegal order is refuted and reduced to a Hold, an annihilator-to-default move. Because the engine is an oracle, board facts can never be contradictory: there is no way to be told two conflicting truths about whether Paris holds an army. So board-state claims live in the consistent fragment of Belnap: they range over \(\{\mathbf{T},\mathbf{N}\}\) (and \(\mathbf{F}\) for a refuted order) but never reach the glut \(\mathbf{B}\). The substrate is the glut-free corner of the bilattice (it keeps the gap \(\mathbf{N}\) and the refuted \(\mathbf{F}\) but never the glut \(\mathbf{B}\)), so it never exercises the contradiction-handling the rest of the algebra adds.

9.2 · Negotiation—the trust model is a graded bilattice value

A Deal ("I support your move to Galicia if you move there too") is a promise: a claim whose verification is deferred to adjudication. At assertion time it is UNRESOLVED-as-fact, and the algebra structurally refuses to let cheap talk pose as a board fact. What a power attaches to it is a trust grade, and that grade is not a number bolted on from outside the algebra.

Subjective logic embeds in the graded bilattice. A subjective-logic opinion is a triple (belief, disbelief, uncertainty) with belief + disbelief + uncertainty = 1. Read it as a graded Belnap value: belief is the for-channel \(a^{+}\), disbelief is the against-channel \(a^{-}\), and uncertainty measures gap-ness (proximity to \(\mathbf{N}\)). The opinion occupies the consistent simplex \(a^{+}+a^{-}\le 1\); the glut \(\mathbf{B}=(\top,\top)\) sits outside it, so the bilattice extends subjective logic with a contradiction a single opinion cannot carry. Trust over "will this power keep its word?" is therefore not outside the algebra; it is the algebra's own graded form, and a betrayal pushes the value past the simplex into the glut. The reputation state and the claim grade are the same kind of object.

Two consequences follow:

A broken promise is a glut. When a power promises to support your move, that pledge enters the for-channel of a claim about their cooperation. When the adjudicator later reveals they did the opposite, the betrayal enters the against-channel. Corroborating the two with \(\oplus_k\) climbs to \((\top,\top)=\mathbf{B}\): you were told both, that they would and that they demonstrably did not. A broken promise is not a gap in your knowledge of them; it is a glut, and the algebra records it as one.

Reputation update is motion in the knowledge order. Each adjudicated round pours evidence into one channel or the other, moving a power's standing up \(\le_k\): toward \(\mathbf{T}\) (reliably keeps promises), toward \(\mathbf{F}\) (reliably defects), or toward \(\mathbf{B}\) (says one thing, does another). A "known double-crosser" is a power whose accumulated con-mass and glut history dominate; a value in the algebra, not an annotation beside it.

9.3 · Where the boundary sits

The boundary sits inward, but it does not vanish. What is native is the representation of trust: a power's reliability, the distinction between "no read on them yet" (gap) and "caught them lying" (glut), and the accumulation of evidence as the game proceeds, all of it living in the algebra as graded Belnap values, and the whole game stays auditable and replayable, since the transcript is a provenance DAG. What remains outside is the selection of action: whether to keep your own promise, whom to deceive, when to defect, a strategic choice under incentives, which is game theory and opponent modeling, not algebra. The algebra shrinks the attack surface to exactly that strategic layer where deception belongs: a power can lie in negotiation, but it cannot lie about the board or make an illegal move. A useful way to state CICERO-style honesty in these terms is to keep your own for-channel consistent with your adjudicated actions, so that no glut ever accrues against you, which is how Meta's CICERO reached human-level play, keeping its messages consistent with its planned moves [29, 30]. The algebra can hold and propagate the trust state; it cannot tell you whether to be trustworthy.

10 · Where the algebra stops

A claim algebra makes the structural failure modes (silent error propagation, missing-value laundering, untracked provenance, and contradiction-masquerading-as-ignorance) into type errors and algebraic impossibilities. It does not make the statistical and strategic problems go away. The accounting has two halves: what the bilattice costs over a single semiring, and the limits that stand regardless, each a layer one would add deliberately rather than a flaw to paper over.

What the two-channel object buys. A purely positive (single-bottom) scheme models only monotone composition: a contradiction collapses to the same bottom as the unknown, and an amendment that supersedes a base clause is a retraction it has no way to express. The bilattice resolves both. Gap (\(\mathbf{N}\)) and glut (\(\mathbf{B}\)) are distinct values; negation and the con-channel express refutation; and a superseding amendment is a retraction (§6) rather than a silent overwrite. The positive/monotone restriction is lifted. It is lifted at a price, stated next.

The limitations that still stand.

Correlation blindness, now sharper. The homomorphic story assumes the leaves are independent; the moment two sources draw on the same upstream document, multiplying their confidences double-counts. Tracking token identity is the fix (probabilistic databases, where exact confidence is \(\#\mathrm{P}\)-hard in general [17]), or moving to Dempster–Shafer belief functions or Jøsang's subjective logic [12, 13], which carry an explicit conflict/uncertainty mass. The bilattice makes this worse to ignore, not better: a glut computed from a for-source and an against-source that secretly share a cause is a spurious contradiction, and an apparent corroboration may be one document echoing itself. Faithful handling needs the provenance to carry the correlation structure and the valuation to live in a domain that respects it, which is exactly the \(\#\mathrm{P}\)-hard regime above. Idempotent \(L\) dodges double-counting an identical leaf but not a latent common cause. The credit topology has an in-network instance: EBITDA fans out to both ratios, so the covenant's pro-lineage is \(\textit{debt}\cdot\textit{ebitda}^{2}\cdot\textit{interest}\), and a non-idempotent render (Viterbi) squares EBITDA's confidence—faithful \(\mathbb{N}[X]\) behavior the idempotent fuzzy render hides. The experiment's headline rides the structural corner and an idempotent declared model, so the squaring is a measured artifact of the non-idempotent renders, not a defect.

Calibration is exogenous. The algebra propagates confidence soundly given the leaf numbers; it does not produce them. Whether "High" on an extraction corresponds to anything in the world is an empirical calibration problem, upstream of the algebra and unchanged by it [16].

Semantic correctness is not verification, and verification is not Belnap's axis. verify() checks that a value faithfully reproduces its cited provenance, not that the value is true: a faithful citation of a wrong source passes. And, as §4.4 insists, verification is a third axis (does-it-reproduce-the-source), distinct from Belnap's evidential axis of told-true/told-false content. Conflating the two is the error this object most wants not to repeat one level up.

Time, retraction, and aggregates. The structure is most natural for a single timeless resolution over an acyclic provenance. Retraction is now partly handled (negation expresses supersession at a point in time), but genuinely temporal belief revision (incremental recomputation as evidence arrives and is withdrawn) and feedback cycles still need iterative fixed-point machinery [14], and quantitative aggregates ("the average across these claims") still call for a semimodule structure over the bilattice [15] rather than the lattice operations alone.

Capacity contention (routing). The §8 shortest-path routes one unit at a fixed cost. The moment two shipments contend for one lane's finite capacity, the problem becomes min-cost multi-commodity flow [27], a linear program or an auction, not a single semiring path. The Reservable(CapacityToken) field only marks the seam; it does not allocate.

The contribution, stated precisely. The mathematics is decades old: Belnap's four values (1977), Dunn's two-channel relational semantics, the Ginsberg–Fitting theory of bilattices and the twist representation, the Green–Karvounarakis–Tannen provenance semiring, and subjective logic. None of it is new. The claim is that the right wire format between LLM agents is a graded bilattice Claim: a two-channel, gap-distinct-from-glut, negation-closed value, fail-closed by the knowledge-meet annihilator, made trust-pluggable by a per-channel homomorphism and verifiable by an orthogonal predicate. Committing to this object turns a list of correctness hopes into a list of theorems and type errors, with contradiction now among the things the type system can see rather than the things it silently drops. The value concentrates where verification is the binding constraint (high-stakes, auditable, regulated work), and that is the wedge worth taking.

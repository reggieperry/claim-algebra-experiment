How to read this

Each ledger entry applies algebra operations to one of six tracked claims: assert / corroborate add a provenance token to a candidate's pro-channel, refute adds to the con-channel, strike withdraws an assertion (the token is cleared from the live channels and retained on the record as a struck trace), and supersede lets a later position govern while the prior is struck and kept as a citable trace. Stepping is a pure re-fold of the event prefix, so back and forward are exact. This per-token strike is the algebra's WithdrawToken(l) — the token-scoped withdrawal of calculus Definition 2.16, which retracts one assertion (the Tsar's countermanded order) while rival tokens (the partial-mobilization intel) stand, leaving no con-residue; it is distinct from a whole-slot strike, which moves a slot's support to the con-channel so it gluts forever.

On the diamond, a marker's position is the rendered grade — a Count-carrier ν̂ over the token stacks — while its corner badge is the structural presence read (T pro-only, F con-only, N neither, B both). Rival positive candidates are Ambiguity (cardinality ≥ 2), never a glut: the glut takes con-channel mass. The gate signs only on corner T with exactly one candidate — this replay trivializes the full gate's other two conjuncts (the render threshold θ and verification), so accept reduces to corner = T ∧ cardinality = 1. A CWS flag marks the moments history signed against a blocked gate — a gate-override. (In the falsification experiment, CWS counts wrong values the gate itself accepts; here the gate is advisory, and sovereigns overrule it.) Keys: ← → step.

Scope & sources

This is an epistemics replay — the July Crisis read through one formal lens, as a demonstration of the ledger’s states and operations. It is not a causal thesis about the war’s origins: contingency, agency, and structure all did their work in 1914, and the historiographical debates (Fischer to Clark) are untouched here. What the replay claims is narrower — that the epistemic states the actors decided from have names, and that the record supports the names given.

Event sequence and particulars follow the standard accounts — Albertini, The Origins of the War of 1914; Clark, The Sleepwalkers; MacMillan, The War That Ended Peace. The operations are those of the companion claim-calculus note. One deliberate triviality: ν̂ here counts tokens and weights no source above another — Vienna corroborating its own reading raises its own grade — which is exactly the trust-model axis July 1914 most strains, and the axis the algebra reserves for per-channel valuations.

# How cutting-edge is the verifiable claim algebra? — research results (Rev 2, bilattice-completed)

A web-grounded assessment of the novelty of the claim algebra, re-established against the Belnap-completed revision: the proposal that every message between LLM agents (and between a model and a human) be a typed `Claim[K, A]` / `Testimony[K, A]` — a value annotated by an element of a graded *distributive bilattice* (Belnap's four values, realized as two evidential channels — support-for and support-against — over the free provenance semiring) carried with two-channel provenance, a graded confidence, and a verification predicate — with fail-closed semantics (the knowledge-meet annihilator), pluggable trust via per-channel provenance homomorphisms through the twist, and verification as a *third* axis orthogonal to both the confidence grade and the evidential for/against pair.

## Method and scope — two sweeps, two registers

The Rev-1 assessment (June 2026) was established against the *semiring* framing: four independent agents searched math foundations, the agent-protocol and trust landscape, multi-agent-LLM reliability, and verifiable/uncertainty-aware output, fetched primary sources, and verified citations. That sweep searched the single-channel positive-semiring object — and never ran the bilattice register.

This update (2026-06-23) re-searches the framing Rev 2 introduced: bilattice, four-valued, paraconsistent, and subjective-logic-graded. The revision matters because subjective logic — now *inside* the algebra as its graded `[0,1]²` form — carries a mature multi-agent trust-fusion literature, and paraconsistent/bilattice logic carries a mature gap-versus-glut literature, that the semiring search could not surface. Every citation reported here was verified — arXiv ID, venue, year, authors — by fetch or search against arXiv, ACM, Springer, and MDPI; no identifier was composed from memory, and the three the citation pass found misstated on author detail are corrected in the references.

**The evidence-of-absence finding, stated explicitly.** The June-2026 survey of execution provenance in LLM agents — Wang et al., arXiv:2606.04990 — maps exactly Rev 2's problem space and lists `Contradict` and `Invalidate` as first-class provenance *relations*, yet catalogs no work using semiring, bilattice, or four-valued logic for message grades, no gap-distinct-from-glut typed value, and no fail-closed-by-construction wire invariant; contradiction lives only as a graph edge. Direct searches for the bilattice-plus-provenance-plus-agent-protocol combination return a void. This is evidence of absence, not proof — the standard caveat below holds — but it is the decisive negative result on the inter-agent axis.

## Headline verdict

**The verdict holds and is reinforced: a novel *synthesis of old parts*.** Moving from semiring to graded distributive bilattice does not weaken the claim — it strengthens it, because Rev 2 now openly bridges *more* disjoint fields into one typed inter-agent wire object: provenance-with-negation (database theory), paraconsistent/Belnap logic, *and* subjective-logic trust fusion. The adversarial bilattice sweep — the register the Rev-1 semiring search never ran — surfaced no work occupying the locus that matters: a graded-bilattice value carried as the typed, fail-closed, trust-pluggable *inter-agent message*.

What changed materially is the moat, not the verdict. Gap-distinct-from-glut and fail-closed are no longer clean differentiators in the abstract: Koomullil's *Proof-Carrying Certificates for LLM Pipelines* (arXiv:2605.16407, May 2026) independently lands both — plus verification-separate-from-confidence — with an LLM, machine-checked in Lean 4. The differentiator must therefore re-pin from "gap≠glut and fail-closed are novel" to the pairing that remains unclaimed: a graded-bilattice value as the typed inter-agent wire contract, made trust-pluggable by a *per-channel* provenance-semiring homomorphism through the twist. Gap≠glut, fail-closed, and verification-as-a-third-axis are necessary ingredients, not the moat.

The qualification that matters: this is a **contested 2026 frontier, not a blue ocean.** Several 2026 papers independently touch two or three components; one touches all three of the bilattice axes at once. The window in which the *full* synthesis is unclaimed is real but plausibly closing.

**The inherited error, corrected.** Rev 1 — and the Rev-1 novelty doc after it — called verification "a *second* axis orthogonal to confidence, the bilattice information-vs-truth structure." That conflated two different things. Belnap's two orders are *truth* (false→true) and *knowledge* (gap→glut), and both are evidential. Verification — does the value recompute against its cited provenance — is *neither*; it is a third axis. Rev 2 makes the for/against evidential pair *be* the Belnap value (the two-channel structure) and keeps verification as a separate, decidable predicate that refines any Belnap value. Koomullil's certificate architecture also separates verification from confidence, so this correctness is not itself a differentiator — it is table stakes the doc had previously gotten wrong.

## What the field does instead — and why it isn't this

The motivating problem is hot ("verification, not generation, is the bottleneck" is a 2026 thesis), but every solution shape differs from a fail-closed, bilattice-graded wire contract:

- **Single-agent bilattice trust gates.** Koomullil's *Proof-Carrying Certificates* (2605.16407) classifies each claim over Belnap `{Supported, Contradicted, Contested, Unknown}` and blocks emission below a proven, fail-closed threshold. It is a pre-emission gate that computes the value *locally* as a certificate — the bilattice value never travels between agents, and there is no provenance-semiring homomorphism for pluggable trust.
- **Belnap reasoning grounded in an LLM.** Allen et al. (2507.09751) ground a sound-and-complete four-valued reasoner in LLM interpretations, with gaps and gluts distinct ("92 gluts" in a medication-safety KB). Single-agent tableau reasoning, no wire format, no provenance algebra.
- **Bilattice multi-source fusion, classic.** Bruns & Huth's Belnap access control (TISSEC 2011) and the PAL2v network-routing line (Sensors 2021) keep gap (unspecified) and glut (conflict) distinct and compose from multiple sources via bilattice meet/join — but as a policy or node-internal *evaluation* value, with no provenance lineage, homomorphism, verification, or agent setting.
- **Subjective-logic trust fusion.** Jøsang trust networks (TNA-SL) and EBSL (1402.3319) carry the exact (belief, disbelief, uncertainty) triple Rev 2 absorbs, and EBSL routes *evidence* through the graph then reads off trust — the per-channel-homomorphism instinct. But the opinion *is* the value: reputation derivation, no payload, no verify()-against-provenance, no glut as a named pole (conflict is normalized away), not a wire contract.
- **Inter-agent conflict detectors.** Acharya's *Semantic Consensus* (2604.16339) lands the algebra's own glut example — "A approves while B rejects the same loan" — and blocks it, as a postcondition-incompatibility detector with a confidence node-attribute; Co-Sight (2510.21557) allocates compute to disagreement hotspots. Detection and reconciliation, not a typed propagating value distinct from ignorance.
- **Identity attestation and audit.** LDP (2603.18043), Proof-Carrying Numbers (2509.06902), and *From Fluent to Verifiable* (2602.13855) secure the *envelope* or audit the *claim* — claimed-vs-attested identity, re-executable numeric provenance, claim-level provenance graphs recording conflicts. None carries gap≠glut as an algebraic value over a semiring with a cross-hop annihilator.
- **Provenance over agent messages, surveyed.** Wang et al. (2606.04990) define execution provenance with `Contradict`/`Invalidate` relations but no semiring, bilattice, or four-valued message grade — contradiction is a graph edge, never a Belnap value on the wire.

## The distinctive moves (the moat), re-pinned

The differentiator is now the *pairing*, not any single ingredient. Four moves, the first two of which carry the load:

1. **A graded-bilattice value as the typed inter-agent wire contract.** The bilattice value travels between agents as the message, fail-closed by the knowledge-meet annihilator `N ⊗ₖ x = N` propagating across arbitrarily many hops — the field *detects, scores, reconciles, or gates locally*; the claim algebra carries the typed value on the wire and prevents laundering by construction. No catalogued agent work does this (2606.04990 is the evidence of absence).
2. **Per-channel homomorphism via the twist, for pluggable trust.** A bilattice is not a semiring, so the GKT homomorphism does not apply once — but via the twist `FOUR ≅ TWO ⊙ TWO` it applies *twice*: a pro-lineage `p⁺` and a con-lineage `p⁻`, each evaluated by its own homomorphism into the underlying lattice `L`. "Compute provenance once, evaluate many trust models" survives per channel. This is a genuine cost, not a free lunch — two polynomials, two evaluations — and EBSL's evidence-then-derive-trust is the nearest cousin, but stated there as an aggregation procedure, never as a free-object universal property or a wire contract.
3. **The for/against bilattice value: gap ≠ glut, fail-closed by the knowledge-meet.** "Told nothing" (`N`) and "told both" (`B`) are distinct values that propagate differently and license different downstream actions (search vs. reconcile/escalate). Necessary, but **not novel in the LLM space** — Koomullil (2605.16407) has gap≠glut and a fail-closed gate, with an LLM, machine-checked.
4. **Verification as a separate orthogonal predicate.** A decidable recompute-against-provenance check, a *third* axis distinct from both the confidence grade and the for/against pair. Also necessary and also **not a differentiator** — Koomullil separates verification from confidence too.

The synthesis is the contribution: moves 3 and 4 are settled ingredients; move 1 paired with move 2 is the locus the bilattice sweep confirms is unclaimed.

## Honest caveats

- **"Found nothing" is evidence of absence, not proof.** Two sweeps in two registers still have coverage and recency limits; a fast-moving 2026 frontier can hold unindexed, very recent, or non-English work.
- **Contested edge, not empty field.** Koomullil (2605.16407) lands all three bilattice axes together, with an LLM, machine-checked. The differentiator is narrower than Rev 1 claimed, and the window is plausibly closing.
- **The bilattice components are individually well-trodden.** Gap≠glut and multi-source fusion go back to Bruns & Huth (2011) and the PAL-routing line; two-channel for/against provenance is Grädel & Tannen's named construct; the (belief, disbelief, uncertainty) triple is two decades of subjective logic. The originality is the synthesis and the inter-agent locus, not any ingredient.
- **Grading carries a cost.** The `[0,1]²` instance makes exact inference under shared evidence #P-hard, and a glut from sources that secretly share a latent cause can be spurious — a sharpening of the correlation-blindness limit, inherited, not cured.

## Implication

The contribution is real, distinctive, and time-sensitive — and the genuinely original part is the *synthesis*: a graded-bilattice value carried as the typed inter-agent wire contract, made trust-pluggable by a per-channel provenance homomorphism through the twist. The bilattice re-search did not kill the claim; it narrowed and better-evidenced it. Gap≠glut, fail-closed, and verification-separate-from-confidence each now have strong independent prior art — Koomullil, Allen, Bruns & Huth, Grädel & Tannen, the subjective-logic cluster — so the moat must rest on the pairing, not on any ingredient. The closest neighbors still have **no clean number attributing a confident-wrong (or contradiction-laundered) reduction to the wire format itself.** The falsification experiment (`docs/claim-algebra/falsification-experiment.md`) is therefore the one result that would empirically differentiate this work from the convergent crowd, and the case for running it — and running it soon — is stronger under Rev 2 than it was under Rev 1.

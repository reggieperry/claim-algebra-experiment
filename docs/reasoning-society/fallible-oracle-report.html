When the checker is wrong, where does the loss go?

A controlled study of fail-open risk in AI agent self-verification — and why stacking redundant checks only buys safety when the checks are independent.

Executive summary

Agent systems increasingly trust automated checks — a test suite, a type checker, one model reviewing another. Every checker is itself fallible, and the premise those systems rest on (a passing check means the work is correct) is exactly what breaks when the check errs.

We built a bench where a multi-agent society concludes a task only through a gate, degraded the checker parametrically, and measured where the loss goes. The architecture is broadly graceful: as the checker fails, the loss goes overwhelmingly to abstention, not to wrong signatures. The wrong signatures that do occur trace to a monoculture at two levels: agents that share blind spots agreeing on the same wrong answer, and redundant confirmations that share a checker failing together. Requiring k confirmations suppresses the second, but only when the confirmations are independent. Since N Claude-based checks are correlated by construction, the transferable lever is check diversity, not quantity. The measurement bears this out (a second same-model check is barely independent), and the weak sign path can be closed outright with a non-generative check — dropping the perfect-oracle wrong-sign rate to zero without sacrificing the correct signs, which re-route to ground truth and survive the closure. That last cell also retires an earlier worry that the win rate and the wrong signatures were coupled: they were separable, and the coupling was an artifact of the open seam.

01

The question

A coding agent runs tests. A reviewer agent reads a diff. One model checks another's output. Each of these is a checker, and each is fallible: a test suite has coverage gaps, a type checker is partial, a model hallucinates. Systems are built on the premise that a passing check licenses a conclusion — and that premise is precisely what fails when the checker is wrong.

The question this bench isolates is narrow and answerable: when the check errs, where does the loss go? Two outcomes are possible, and they are not equally bad. The system can abstain: decline to conclude, and hand the decision back. Or it can fail open — emit a confident, wrong signature that a correct check would never have permitted. Abstention is recoverable; a fail-open is a wrong answer wearing the badge of a verified one.

Stated as a falsifiable pair: under H-graceful, falling checker reliability is absorbed by abstention and the fail-open rate stays near zero; under H-catastrophic, the loss passes straight through and the fail-open rate tracks the checker's error rate. The bench is built to tell these apart, and to find which of the architecture's guards moves the slope between them: the corroboration floor, the skeptic agent, or redundant confirmation.

02

The apparatus

We instrumented a small multi-agent society playing Twenty Questions. A cohort of Claude Haiku 4.5 agents proposes and narrows hypotheses about a hidden target; an oracle answers the society's yes/no questions. The society may sign a final answer only through a gate. The gate reads a typed, four-valued (Belnap) evidence algebra and signs one hypothesis only when the evidence is structurally clean:

accept  ⇔  corner = True  ∧  cardinality = 1  ∧  ( backers ≥ 2  ∨  confirmations ≥ k )

A conclusion signs only when exactly one hypothesis stands, unrefuted (corner=True, cardinality=1), and is backed either by two independent agents or by k ground-truth confirmations. The confirmation quorum k is the lever this experiment turns.

The target is sealed outside the agents' view. A mechanical adjudicator (no model in the loop) classifies every finished game against it, into exactly one of three outcomes:

Everything downstream reduces to counting fail-opens as the checker is degraded.

03

The fail-open is real

We degraded the oracle to 70% reliability with a systematic error profile — wrong on a fixed subset of questions, and consistently wrong on re-ask, the way a checker with a genuine blind spot behaves. Running live against Claude Haiku 4.5, the fail-open appeared. The sealed target was apple; the society signed crystal_vase.

The trace is the point. The society did not behave erratically — it reasoned correctly from poisoned premises:

A fail-open needs two things to go wrong at once: the society must pose a wrong guess, and the checker must confirm it. Formally, P(fail-open) = P(wrong guess) × P(confirmed | wrong guess), and the trace shows both factors firing. Reading these logs, not the summary rate, is how the program finds its failure classes — every fail-open is fully replayable, and each of this project's known failure modes came from reading one.

04

The primary result — graceful, with a floor

We swept oracle reliability under the systematic error model, live, over a difficulty-mixed target set. The run is small (12 games per cell, wide intervals); its point is a verdict on the axis, not a precise slope. As the checker degrades, does the loss go to abstention or to fail-open?

Overwhelmingly to abstention. The dominant outcome at every reliability is a held conclusion (0.67 to 1.00) — the architecture declines far more often than it signs wrong. That is the graceful side of the pair, and the part of the run that survives its wide intervals.

The fail-opens that remain have two distinct sources, and the gate's own sign-path attribution separates them. A conclusion can sign two ways: on two agents agreeing, or on the oracle confirming a guess. Splitting the fail-open by which path it took is the finding.

At a perfect oracle the fail-open is entirely the two-agent path, and this is deductive rather than merely observed: a wrong guess at a perfect oracle is answered “no” and struck, so an oracle-confirmed wrong sign cannot occur there. The floor is two Haiku agents agreeing on a wrong candidate through the corroboration path, which never consults the oracle. At a degraded oracle the fail-open flips to entirely the oracle-confirmed path: the corrupted guess-confirmations of the previous section.

The finding

The corroboration floor is the monoculture at the agent level. “Two independent backers” is only as strong as the agents' independence, and two instances of one model are not independent — they share blind spots and agree on the same wrong answer, unchecked.

Stated in the gate's terms, the finding is exact. The last requirement of the accept rule is a disjunction (sign on two backers, or on k confirmations), and a disjunction is only as strong as its weakest satisfiable branch. Oracle confirmation is strong; two-agent agreement is weak, because it can hold when the candidate is false. So the gate signs through the weak branch even where the strong one would have caught the error, and the useful question stops being “is the gate fail-closed?” It becomes: what does each sign path trust, and can that thing be wrong? Fail-closedness is a property of a path, and the system inherits the weakest one.

Sign-correct is zero across the sweep: the Haiku society rarely narrows to the true target for these hard targets, so the high abstention is partly the model's difficulty with the task, not only the architecture. The architecture-level readings do not depend on that difficulty: abstention dominance, and the two-source split of the fail-open.

05

The mitigation

The natural defense is redundancy: before signing, require k confirmations rather than one. We threaded a confirmation quorum through the gate — a change to the fail-closed core, the one part of the system whose correctness a wrong signature depends on. The discipline matched the stakes: a design committee revised the approach before a line was written, and an independent adversarial review then tried and failed to find a path that signs below quorum.

The change is conservative by construction. At k = 1 it is byte-identical to the shipped gate — a single confirmation still signs, exactly as before. Only k > 1 tightens the last conjunct, and only the experiment sets it. The re-pose loop is bounded and aborts on the first non-confirmation, so it cannot spin or sign on a stale reply.

06

The correlation result — independence, not quantity

The quorum targets one of the two fail-open sources: the oracle-confirmed path, the one that grows as the checker degrades. It raises a question redundancy usually gets to dodge — are the k confirmations independent? We made that a dial. Sweeping the quorum k against the correlation ρ between confirmations produces the surface in Figure 1, and it has two edges that tell the whole story.

Along the independent edge (ρ=0), a fail-open requires every one of the k confirmations to be independently wrong, so its rate falls geometrically: 0.32 at k=1, 0.09 at k=2, 0.02 at k=3. Redundancy compounds. Along the correlated edge (ρ=1), the confirmations err together, so any number of them is exactly as reliable as one — every line returns to the single-check ceiling of 0.30. Redundancy buys nothing.

The finding

Redundancy pays for independence, not for quantity. Three correlated confirmations are no safer than one.

The real ρ, measured

The curve's ρ was dialed. In a separate live experiment we measured the real error correlation between confirmers, on a set of hard questions where capable models actually err. Two instances of the same model err together far more than independence would predict; a different model family helps but does not make them independent; only a non-generative check breaks the correlation entirely.

Diversity of model is not diversity of error. Even different capable model families are correlated on the hard cases where a check matters, so the only genuinely independent check is a non-generative one — a test, a proof, a lookup, a human. That is the project's founding premise, now measured.

07

Holding gaps closed

A degraded checker does not only lie; sometimes it says “I don't know.” The gate has to treat that gap correctly: a “don't know” on the question a conclusion depends on must force abstention, and a gap elsewhere must not block a sound conclusion. This is a correctness property the gate was built to satisfy, exercised in the running system here for the first time, with an oracle that returns a genuine gap on a chosen class of question.

Both directions hold. An on-path gap (a gap on the guess the sign depends on) forces abstention, never a signature, even when the guess is in fact correct — the gate declines rather than sign on a question it could not verify. An off-path gap (a gap on a belief-inert property answer) does not block; the society routes around it and signs. A gap stops a conclusion exactly when it lands on the question the conclusion rests on, and nowhere else.

This is the failure mode most easily gotten wrong in the safe direction: over-propagate the gap and the gate abstains on everything, safe but useless. The path-local behavior is what keeps “fail-closed” from collapsing into “never concludes.”

08

Closing the seam

The primary result found a hole: the accept rule signs through a weak branch that never consults ground truth. A finding like that invites its own completion — close the hole and measure the closure.

The fix follows the standing rule: never lower a floor, add a check. We gated the corroboration path so a two-agent agreement can no longer sign on its own; it must obtain a ground-truth confirmation first, exactly as a lone candidate does. In the algebra this narrows the disjunction toward a single checked path. It is a change to the fail-closed core, so it went through the same discipline as the mitigation: a design committee, then an independent adversarial review that traced every sign site and returned merge-safe.

At a perfect oracle, closing the seam drops the fail-open from 0.25 to 0.00. The three wrong two-agent signs become abstentions. And the one correct sign survives, which is the control that matters: if the demoted candidates were quietly dropped rather than checked, the correct sign would have vanished too. It did not, so the candidates are genuinely reaching ground truth — the wrong ones are answered “no” and held, the right one is confirmed and signs.

Where the two threads meet

The check that closes the seam is the ground-truth oracle, and ground truth is non-generative — exactly the one confirmer the correlation measurement found to be genuinely independent. The hole is closed by the only kind of check that is not itself a monoculture.

09

Scope and limits

The correlation curve invites a stronger claim than it supports. An earlier draft called this the monoculture failure mode “measured”; an internal review flagged that as an overclaim, and it was right. In the curve, ρ is dialed, not estimated — the curve demonstrates what a given correlation does. (The real ρ is measured separately, above; the curve itself still only dials it.)

What it is

  • An end-to-end demonstration that the k-quorum machinery behaves as designed through the real gate and re-pose loop — a buggy loop would break the curve.
  • The monoculture failure mode made concrete as a function of a given confirmation correlation.
  • Deterministic, hermetic, and reproducible; verified by committee and by an adversarial review that itself is not a rig (no arm comparison, no control to strawman).

What it is not

  • A measurement of real check correlation from the curve — there ρ is dialed. A separate experiment does estimate it (same-model ρ near 0.75).
  • A numerical discovery — the curve equals a closed form that a pure unit test already pins. The value there is integration confidence.
  • Evidence that redundancy helps in practice at any single reliability; the primary sweep is small, its intervals wide.

The untested half

One limit governs how far “graceful” can be pushed. Sign-correct was zero at every cell, including at a perfect oracle on an easy target, so the bench tested only half of fail-closed: it abstains when unsure, but it was never shown to sign correctly when sure. A separate diagnostic settled why — the society reaches a guess only a quarter of the time and never the correct one, and loosening the round budget does not change that. The endgame and search are structurally weak at this model tier, not merely starved. Until that is fixed, “does not lie” is the strongest reading the graceful result supports, and a wider reliability sweep would only measure the degradation of a system that does not yet succeed.

Trying to fix it, and what the attempt revealed

Reading the transcripts changed the diagnosis: the society is a competent player mis-served by the apparatus, not a broken reasoner. It converged on “domestic dog” for the target “dog” and the exact-string grader scored it wrong; the truth oracle hedged “unknown” on answerable category questions; the agents could not see how many questions remained, so they never committed. Three corrections followed: a semantic grader that accepts a refined phrasing, a decisive oracle, and a visible round budget with a nudge to commit before it runs out.

The honest outcome is an improvement, not a fix. Sign-correct moved from zero to about 0.15–0.19, and that gain is not a grading artifact — on ten held-out targets never used while building the fixes, every win signed the exact target word, and an adversarial review found the semantic grader conservative, declining “green apple” and “guide dog” and never crediting a wrong thing. But seam-open — the configuration measured here — when the society commits it signs wrong about as often as right, and at a degraded oracle the win rate falls back to zero. The first half of that turns out to be an artifact of the open seam, resolved next; the second is the genuine remaining limit.

The coupling was a seam-open artifact

That reading — wins and confident-wrongs coupled through the commit nudge — rested on an inference across two runs with different conditions: the improved endgame was measured with the seam open, while the seam closure was measured earlier on a different, un-improved system, on different targets. The program does not accept a conclusion stitched that way. The composed cell runs the one corner never measured — the improved endgame with the seam gated, at a perfect oracle, on the improvement's own targets — and reruns the seam-open arm in the same invocation as a within-run control.

The coupling dissolves

The confident-wrongs were the open seam, not the commit nudge. Seam-open, every one of the fifteen wrong signs is a two-agent agreement the oracle never checks; gating routes them to ground truth, where the wrong ones are answered “no” and only the right ones sign. The nudge produces the wins — through the oracle — and those survive. Wins and confident-wrongs are separable, not coupled: at perfect check reliability the gated society wins and never lies, by the deduction and by measurement. What stays open is capability under degradation — a reasoner question, not an architecture one.

A consequence: the fully-measured verification-spend tradeoff has no live signal. Gating the confirmation as the sole sign path at a degraded oracle, the improved system signs nothing at all, so there is no fail-open and no useful-yield to weigh across spending policies. The tradeoff's clean form stays the hermetic correlation curve read at the measured correlation — two same-family confirmations buy almost nothing, an independent check approaches the geometric floor.

10

Verification economics, and what remains

The arm closest to real Claude Code asks where to spend a fixed verification budget: on search (more questions, so a wrong guess is posed less often) or on confirmation (a higher quorum, so a wrong guess is confirmed less often). The results point its direction, though not its magnitude.

One sign path is beyond any guess-confirmation budget: the 2-backer path never consults the oracle, so spending there cannot touch it. How much of the fail-open lives on that path this run does not establish — the sampled cells put every fail-open cleanly on one path or the other (all 2-backer at a perfect oracle, all oracle-confirmed at a degraded one), and the good-but-imperfect band where the two compete was never sampled. What the run does show is that the part a budget can reach, the oracle-confirmed fail-open, barely benefits from more confirmations once they are correlated.

The direction follows from the seam: a fixed budget is better spent on an independent check of the corroboration path than on re-confirming the same guess with the same kind of checker. Pricing it precisely was the object of a live spend sweep on the winning system (§09), but the endgame improvement does not win reliably enough at a degraded oracle to sign anything under a gated confirmation, so that sweep returned no signal. The tradeoff's clean form stays the hermetic correlation curve read at the measured correlation; a live spend-per-fail-open measurement waits on a stronger reasoner.

11

Why it matters for Anthropic

Claude Code already self-verifies, and multi-agent products multiply the pattern: agents proposing, reviewing, and confirming one another's work. This bench qualifies the redundancy intuition — add another check, and the odds of a wrong sign multiply down.

The constraint is direct, and the bench shows it at two levels. Correlated checks do not deliver the geometric safety the intuition promises, and N Claude-based checks are correlated by construction. It appears in the confirmation quorum, where redundancy flattens as the confirmations correlate, and one layer down in the corroboration floor, where two Haiku agents sharing blind spots agree on the same wrong answer and sign it — a wrong signature at a perfect oracle, from redundancy that was correlated all along.

Two levers follow, and they are the ones worth designing toward:

Diversify the checkers, and prefer non-generative ones

The safety comes from independent verification paths, not from more instances of the same checker. And the measurement sharpens what “independent” has to mean: a second instance of the same model is barely independent (error correlation near 0.75), a different model family helps but only partway (0.51), and only a non-generative check is genuinely uncorrelated: a test, a proof, a lookup, a human. Diversity of model is not diversity of error; the check worth adding is the one that does not itself hallucinate.

And the seam can be closed. Gating the weak path with such a check drops the perfect-oracle wrong-sign rate to zero without losing the correct ones, so this is a design lever, not just a caution: enumerate the sign paths, and for each require an independent, ideally non-generative, check.

Default to fail-closed

The graceful result rests entirely on a gate that abstains under uncertainty. Abstention is what keeps a wrong check from becoming a wrong signature, and it is the outcome the bench found dominant — worth engineering for even at the cost of throughput.

Method

Three arms, one gate. The primary arm runs live (Haiku society, Sonnet ground truth) because the search degradation as the checker fails cannot be faithfully scripted; a bounded sweep (12 games per cell) places the architecture on the graceful/catastrophic axis and splits each fail-open by sign path. The correlation arm runs hermetic and large-N (800 games per cell): a scripted lone-hypothesis cohort always reaches the guess, the sealed target differs so every signature is a fail-open, and the confirmation is answered through a correlated error model — a deterministic (k, ρ) curve through the real gate and re-pose loop. The partial arm is a hermetic correctness check: an oracle that returns a genuine gap, asserting the gate's gap-only, path-local abstention. The adjudicator is mechanical, no model in the loop; targets are sealed; rates carry Wilson intervals. Four follow-ups extend the arms: an endgame diagnostic (why correct signs are absent), a confirmer-correlation measurement (the real error correlation between two confirmers, same-model and cross-family), the seam closure (a live A/B gating the corroboration path behind a ground-truth check), and the composed cell (the improved endgame under the closed seam, measured within-run at a perfect oracle against a seam-open control). The seam closure is a fail-closed-core change, committee-reviewed and adversarially verified; the composed cell was pre-registered, committee-reconciled, and adversarially verified before the run.
